I've been having trouble writing the post that I want to write, deconstructing and/or recapitulating my gender. There are a number of points to make, each with a mountain of processing needed in order to cover it with satisfactory nuance. It feels unapproachable, but posting it feels like a blocker to posting, or doing, anything else. So instead, I'll just handwave the nuance, and post bullet points. So:

* Vision treatment taught me to hate my body
* People around me taught me to hate myself
* I had an especially hard time with my voice changing, because I hated my body and my self
* Continuing going to church could have helped, if I had talked to someone about why I had stopped singing along
* I was not born transgender; it was a result of these experiences
* I consider my transition unsuccessful, and I think it was so because I had not yet fully identified, let alone dealt with, the causes of my transness
* When I am feeling good, I embrace the autonomy in my body and identity that transition gave me
* When I am not feeling good, I lament that I felt the need to change my body in order to pursue being and improving my self
* It has been necessary to feel the bad feelings to come to a better understanding of the root causes of my problems, but at some point I need to move on
* Singing with my full voice, and debugging why I struggle with it, is an important part of moving on for me
