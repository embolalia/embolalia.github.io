what privilege to gaze upon beauty. to not merely see it, but to give your eyes over to it. to let it speak to you, and let your eyes speak before it, as if it were the River, or the finest window in the cathedral, or the face of a loved one. but alas, I am cursed with an evil eye. were it not for the glass in front of the untitled Rothko, I fear I would vomit upon it.

the glass has its own beauty. it brings reflection. what is it that draws me to this beauty? it cannot be to change, or even touch, or the glass would turn me away. even the beauty is distorted by the glaring glass. what is it that I want to know?
